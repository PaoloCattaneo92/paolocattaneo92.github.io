Our Story

The Saga of Frostlight

Born in the shadow of the Orobie Alps, Frostlight emerged from the ancient city of Bergamo in 2014, united by a shared vision: to forge a sound that channels the raw power of the mountains and the haunting melodies of forgotten legends. Drawing from the deep wells of Northern Italian folklore, Norse mythology, and the untamed spirit of the Alpine wilderness, the band crafts epic soundscapes that bridge the ages.

Their debut album "Echoes of the Frozen Throne" (2017) garnered critical acclaim across the European folk metal scene, praised for its seamless fusion of traditional instruments—hurdy-gurdy, bodhrán, and tin whistle—with crushing guitar riffs and symphonic orchestration. The follow-up, "Hymns of the Iron Peak" (2020), cemented their reputation as one of Italy's most ambitious metal acts, featuring a 40-piece choir on the title track.

With three studio albums, over 200 live performances across Europe, and a devoted following known as "The Frost Legion," Frostlight continues to push the boundaries of epic folk metal. Their latest single "But the Dawn Came Again" heralds a new chapter—darker, heavier, and more cinematic than ever before.
